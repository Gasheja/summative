Climate Change

Understanding the causes, effects, and solutions to one of the biggest global challenges.

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term changes in temperature, precipitation, and other atmospheric patterns. It is primarily driven by human activities such as the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, which increase the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.

Causes of Climate Change

The main cause of climate change is the greenhouse effect. When carbon dioxide (CO2), methane, and other gases accumulate in the atmosphere, they trap heat from the sun, leading to rising global temperatures. Human activities, especially the burning of coal, oil, and natural gas, have greatly increased the concentration of these gases.

Effects of Climate Change

Climate change has widespread impacts on ecosystems, weather patterns, and human communities. Rising sea levels, more frequent extreme weather events, and loss of biodiversity are just some of the major consequences. Vulnerable communities are particularly affected by food insecurity, water scarcity, and displacement.

How Can We Mitigate Climate Change?

Addressing climate change requires global cooperation and action. Mitigation efforts focus on reducing greenhouse gas emissions by transitioning to renewable energy sources like solar and wind, improving energy efficiency, reforestation, and adopting sustainable practices in industries and agriculture.
